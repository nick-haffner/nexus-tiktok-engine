# Nexus Product Context for Social Media

This file captures the product context that should inform copy, captions, CTAs, and any in-content product mentions across all channels. Updated as the product evolves.

## What Is Nexus?

Nexus Concierge is a platform that connects travelers with curated local recommendations from real locals and exclusive partnerships with locally revered businesses. Core narrative: **"Every traveler deserves a local friend."**

- **Founded:** 2024
- **Launch target:** April 14, 2026 (waitlist is live now)
- **Current markets:** Los Angeles and Dallas (local recommendations); Phoenix, LA, Houston (premium services)

## The Product: Local Recommendations Interface (LRI)

Two modes on every market/listing page:

1. **Structured Search** — Filter by group makeup (solo, couple, friends, family), vibe (leisure, celebrating, work, exploring), and time window (daytime, evening, late night). Free-text search: "What are you looking for?" Returns 3-8 curated picks per category (Dining, Bars & Drinks, Activities, Coffee, Views & Spots).
2. **Local Friend AI Chat** — Conversational AI that knows the city. Opens with: "Hey, I'm your friend in [city]. Got any questions?" Shares context with structured search so it knows what you've already seen.

## What Makes It Different (Key Claims for Content)

- **Interview-based recommendations** from diverse locals — not scraped reviews or algorithms.
- **No paid placements.** Partner businesses are surfaced last, after organic local picks. Revenue comes from services, not recommendation placement.
- **42 attributes per recommendation** — vibe, budget, best-for, time of day, etc. Enables natural-language AI queries that feel like asking a friend.
- **Native attribution** — recommendations credited to first name + last initial (e.g., "Sarah M."), humanizing the output.
- **No star ratings or review counts.** Cards read like conversational sentences ("Low-key oyster bar, best seat is at the counter").

## What NOT to Claim

- Don't say Nexus covers every city — it's LA and Dallas at launch.
- Don't promise the AI chat is live yet — it's in development.
- Don't frame premium services as the main product — LRI is the flagship.
- Don't use "travel like a local" as a Nexus tagline — it's the existing TikTok CTA and predates the product. Keep them distinct unless intentionally merging.
