## Design Personality

- **Navy foundation** — formal, trustworthy, premium but not cold.
- **Cyan accent** — modern, vibrant. The "spark" against the navy. Use sparingly.
- **Pill-shaped UI elements** — organic, approachable, not corporate.
- **Compact typography** — mobile-optimized, no wasted space.
- **Light blue warmth** — the background tone that makes navy feel inviting rather than corporate.

# General Social Media Brand Usage

These guidelines apply to any Nexus social media content — TikTok, Instagram, stories, reels, static posts, etc.

## When to Use Brand Colors

The Nexus brand should feel **present but not loud** in content. The content itself (recommendations, local knowledge, city vibes) is the star. The brand supports it.

**Navy (`#010424`)** — Use as:
- Background overlays or gradients behind text (at 30-50% opacity over images, or solid on dedicated brand slides).
- The dominant color whenever the brand is "speaking" (product slides, CTAs, announcements).
- A grounding tone for slides that aren't about a specific place.

**Cyan (`#00E4DA`)** — Use as:
- Small accents: underlines, highlights, emoji glow, a single keyword pop.
- Never as body text color — too low contrast on most backgrounds for legibility.
- The signal that says "this is Nexus" in a sea of white text. A little goes a long way.

**White (`#FFFFFF`)** — Use as:
- Primary text color on any image-backed or dark-backed slide. Always with a heavy outline or shadow for legibility.

## When NOT to Use Brand Colors

- **On editorial/content slides.** When the post is showing recommendations, sharing local knowledge, or telling a story, the brand should recede. White text on imagery, no colored overlays. The content should feel authentic and editorial, not branded.
- **As a substitute for good photography.** A navy overlay doesn't fix a bad image.
- **Excessively.** If every slide is navy and cyan, nothing feels special. Reserve brand colors for the moments where Nexus is speaking as Nexus.

## Logo Usage

- **White logo on dark backgrounds only.** The white logo disappears on light images.
- **Give it space.** Don't crowd the logo with text. It should breathe.
- **Icon vs. wordmark:** Use the icon (`nexus-logo-white.png`) when space is tight. Use the wordmark (`nexus-logo-wordmark-white.png`) when there's room and the viewer may not recognize the icon alone (early-stage brand building).

## The `nexus-concierge.com` Watermark

When the URL appears as a persistent element across multiple slides/frames:
- It should be **subtly branded** — not just plain white text. Options:
  - White text with a slight cyan (`#00E4DA`) tint or glow.
  - White text on a small, semi-transparent navy (`#010424`) pill/badge.
  - White text with a very thin cyan underline.
- The goal: the URL should feel like it belongs to a brand, not like generic watermark text. But it should never compete with the slide's main content.
- **Consistent placement** across all slides where it appears.

## Product & CTA Moments

When the content transitions from editorial to brand (e.g., "we're building something" or "join the waitlist"):
- This is where navy and cyan earn their keep.
- The visual shift from content slides to brand slides should be noticeable but not jarring. A gradient overlay, a subtle color temperature shift, or a background treatment change signals "the brand is talking now."
- **CTA slides** get the strongest brand treatment: prominent logo, navy background or overlay, cyan accent on the action element (URL, "join the waitlist").
