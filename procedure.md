Nexus TikTok Carousel Production Procedure
Overview This document outlines the step-by-step, AI-assisted assembly line for creating Nexus "Local vs. Tourist" TikTok carousels. It is designed to be executed by an AI and reviewed by a human manager at strict approval gates.
The Nexus AI Tool Stack To achieve the best results and respect the limitations of different AI models, this workflow uses specific tools for specific phases:
Phase 1 (Copywriting): ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.
Phase 2 (Visual Sourcing): ChatGPT (with native web search enabled) or Perplexity to source authentic, real-world photos.
Phase 3 (Assembly): Gemini (using the Nano Banana 2 image model) to composite the text onto the final images.
Ultimate Acceptance Criteria (The Final Product)
Before beginning, understand what the final output must achieve:
Tone: Confident, insider, contrarian, and concise.
Structure: Exactly 1 Hook Slide, 3-6 Body Slides, and 1 Call-To-Action (CTA) Slide.
Word Count: Explanations for "Tourist Move" and "Local Move" must not exceed 10 words or 2 short sentences per item.
Visuals: 9:16 vertical images ready for direct TikTok upload. Real photos only (no AI-generated landmarks/storefronts).
Formatting: Highly legible white text with a thick black outline/drop-shadow, centered, respecting TikTok UI safe zones (right side and bottom 20% clear).

Phase 0: Concept, Ideation & Prompt Preparation
The goal here is to establish the core idea, ingest the Nexus database recommendations, and map out the content structure so Phase 1 has all the necessary ingredients.
Phase 0 Acceptance Criteria (Check before Manager Review):
[ ] Is there a single, approved central concept/city for the carousel?
[ ] Has the AI created a high-level outline consisting of 3 to 6 body slides?
[ ] Does every planned slide have both a "Tourist Trap" and a "Local Move" identified (even if roughly stated)?
[ ] Did the AI successfully use web research to fill in any missing gaps (e.g., identifying a tourist trap to contrast with the manager's local recommendation)?
Step 0.1 - Ideation & Selection: The AI generates 3 to 5 high-potential carousel ideas based on the "Local vs. Tourist" or "Worth It" framework (e.g., "Miami: Local vs Tourist," or "Nashville: Overrated vs Underrated"). The manager reviews, inputs their own edits or ideas, and approves a single concept. Step 0.2 - Data Ingestion: The manager inputs the specific local recommendations for the approved concept, pulling from the Nexus database or personal knowledge (e.g., "For Seattle, we need to recommend Taylor Shellfish and Kerry Park"). Step 0.3 - Outline & AI Gap-Filling: The AI creates a brief content overview mapping out the expected slides.
The Gap-Fill Rule: If the manager provided a "Local Move" but no corresponding "Tourist Trap" (or vice versa), the AI must use its web-browsing capabilities to research and propose an accurate, highly relevant counterpart.
The Volume Rule: If the manager provides fewer than 3 recommendations, the AI must research and propose additional local/tourist pairings to ensure the carousel has enough content (3-6 body slides). Manager Review Gate 0: The AI presents the complete slide outline (Concept + List of Tourist/Local pairings) to the manager. The manager reviews, tweaks any locations, and replies with "Outline Approved. Proceed to Phase 1."

Phase 1: Copywriting & Flow Mapping
The goal here is to lock in the exact text for every single slide in a structured format before sourcing images.
Phase 1 Acceptance Criteria (Check before Manager Review):
[ ] Does the output contain exactly 1 Hook, the requested number of Body Slides, and 1 CTA slide?
[ ] Does every Body Slide have exactly one ❌ (Tourist) and one ✅ (Local)?
[ ] Are the explanations for each location strictly 10 words or fewer?
[ ] Is the output formatted in a clean Markdown layout for easy reading?
Step 1.1 - AI Persona & Tone: Act as an expert local travel copywriter for the brand "Nexus." The tone must be authoritative, slightly snarky about tourist traps, and fiercely loyal to local gems. Step 1.2 - The Hook Slide: Draft the title for the first slide using high-contrast language (e.g., "Local vs Tourist in [City] 🤩" or "[City] spots that are actually worth it ✨"). Step 1.3 - The Body Slides: Translate the raw input into the strict Nexus format. You must apply the following constraints to every body slide:
Category Header: Include a relevant emoji and category (e.g., ☕️ Coffee, 🌮 Tex-Mex).
Tourist Move ❌: State the tourist trap. Provide a 1-line, 5-10 word explanation of why to avoid it (e.g., "Long line, same coffee").
Local Move ✅: State the Nexus recommendation. Provide a 1-line, 5-10 word explanation of why it’s better (e.g., "Better beans. Better atmosphere."). Step 1.4 - The CTA Slide: Output the exact final slide text: "Travel like a local. Read more in the caption." Step 1.5 - AI Output Generation: The AI must output the drafted text in a clean code block or Markdown table. Manager Review Gate 1: Present the drafted text to the manager. Ask for approval or revisions. DO NOT proceed to Phase 2 until the manager explicitly types "Approved."

Phase 2: AI Visual Sourcing & Selection
The goal here is to use an AI with live web-browsing capabilities to fetch and display sets of actual images for the manager to choose from.
Phase 2 Acceptance Criteria (Check before Manager Review):
[ ] Are there 3 to 4 distinct, real image options presented for every single slide generated in Phase 1?
[ ] Are the images geographically accurate (e.g., no AI-generated fake landmarks or incorrect storefronts)?
[ ] Are the images portrait-friendly (meaning they can easily be cropped to a 9:16 vertical aspect ratio)?
[ ] Do the images for the "Local Move" look like authentic, user-generated photos rather than overly polished stock photos?
Step 2.1 - AI Search Execution: For every slide approved in Phase 1, the AI must use its web browsing capabilities to fetch 3 to 4 image options per slide. Step 2.2 - Sourcing Rules & Aesthetics:
Hook & CTA Slides: Retrieve iconic, aesthetic establishing shots of the city (e.g., skylines, golden hour landmarks).
Tourist Traps: Retrieve accurate, high-quality images representing the trap.
Local Moves: Retrieve authentic photos of the specific business or location (prioritize user-generated style photos from Google Maps/Yelp).
Generative AI Ban: Do NOT use generative AI (DALL-E, Midjourney, etc.) to create images of specific local businesses or real-world landmarks. Step 2.3 - AI Output Generation: The AI must display the fetched images directly in the chat interface, organized clearly by slide number and concept (e.g., "Slide 2 - Tourist: Pike Place - Option A, B, C"). Manager Review Gate 2: The AI presents the image sets to the manager. The manager reviews, selects the best image for each slide, and manually downloads the chosen images to their local drive to prepare for Phase 3. The manager replies with "Images Selected and Downloaded." DO NOT proceed until the manager gives this approval.

Phase 3: AI Final Rendering & Assembly
The goal here is to use Gemini (Nano Banana 2) to composite the approved text over the downloaded images, resulting in final, TikTok-ready files.
Phase 3 Acceptance Criteria (Check before final sign-off):
[ ] Has the AI successfully merged the approved Phase 1 text onto the provided Phase 2 images in a 9:16 aspect ratio?
[ ] Is the text highly legible, center-aligned, mimicking a bold, white sans-serif font with a dark outline/shadow? (Note: Minor generative artifacts in the font are acceptable, but spelling must be 100% accurate).
[ ] Has the AI respected the TikTok safe zones (leaving the right margin and bottom 20% clear of text)?
[ ] Are the ❌ and ✅ icons clearly identifiable? (Note: If the AI consistently distorts the emojis, omit them from the prompt and add them natively in the TikTok app later).
[ ] Are the Nexus logo (Slides 1 and CTA) and a swipe arrow (Slide 1) present?
Step 3.1 - Prompting the Render: The manager uploads the selected Phase 2 images to Gemini. For each image, Gemini will utilize its image editing capabilities to overlay the exact approved text from Phase 1. Step 3.2 - Applying Design Constraints: Gemini must apply the following constraints to every generation:
Aspect Ratio: Crop or expand the background to exactly 9:16 (Vertical).
Typography Styling: Render text in a bold, sans-serif style ("Classic" TikTok font). The text must be white with a heavy black outline OR a subtle 15-20% dark gradient/drop-shadow behind the text for maximum contrast.
Anti-Jumping: Keep text placement as consistent as possible across all slides (exact X/Y coordinates) to prevent visual jumping when swiping.
Safe Zones: Center the text. Ensure no text bleeds into the bottom 20% or the right-hand edge. Step 3.3 - Slide-Specific Additions: \* Hook Slide (Slide 1): Integrate the Nexus logo clearly. Add a subtle visual cue (like a small arrow pointing right) prompting the user to swipe.
CTA Slide (Final Slide): Integrate the Nexus logo clearly alongside the final text. Manager Review Gate 3: Gemini presents the fully rendered, downloadable images in the chat. The manager does a final QA for typos, text placement, or visual artifacts. If approved, the manager downloads the final images.
\*\*\* Procedure Complete. The files are now ready for manual upload to TikTok (where trending audio and captions will be applied).
