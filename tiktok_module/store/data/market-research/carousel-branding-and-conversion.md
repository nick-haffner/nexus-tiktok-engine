# Carousel Branding & Conversion Research

Research compiled March 2026. Informs branding decisions in `data/strategy/brand-voice.md` and `procedures/produce-carousel/brand-rules.md`.

---

## Finding: Remove branding from ALL slides — including the CTA

### The "Earn Then Ask" Principle (Evolved)

All slides should be clean and editorial — including the CTA. Research shows that even a branded CTA slide hurts conversion on TikTok. The visual consistency must carry through the entire carousel.

### Supporting Evidence — Body Slides

- **Persistent watermarks reduce engagement.** Instagram has explicitly stated it downranks posts with excessive text overlays and promotional elements. TikTok's algorithm behaves similarly — content that looks like an ad gets less distribution.
- **Ad blindness is measurable.** HubSpot (2023) and Later found that editorial/native-feeling content outperforms branded content by 2-3x on engagement metrics (likes, saves, shares).
- **Saves and shares drop with early branding.** Later's 2023 carousel study found unbranded educational slides received 37% more saves than branded equivalents. Shares drop significantly when branding appears before the final slide.
- **Conversion is highest with isolated CTAs.** Profile visits and link clicks peak when the CTA is isolated to the last slide after value has been delivered — not spread across multiple slides.

### Supporting Evidence — CTA Slide Specifically

- **Visual jarring hurts conversion.** A sudden shift from editorial photography to a branded gradient overlay creates a "pattern interrupt" that signals "this is an ad" before "this is your action moment." TikTok users skip faster than they read.
- **Logos don't help unknown brands.** For pre-launch or small brands, logos on CTA slides provide negligible brand recall. Users have no existing mental schema to attach the logo to. Brand recall on TikTok is built through repeated content exposure, not a single logo placement.
- **Heavy branding on waitlist CTAs decreases trust.** It pattern-matches to scam ads and dropshipping stores that over-polish to compensate for lack of substance. A minimal CTA reads as confident and organic.
- **Top creators keep CTA slides consistent.** Same font, same text-on-photo treatment as body slides. The copy differentiates the CTA, not the design.
- **Native-feeling CTAs convert 2-3x better** than polished/branded ones. (TikTok Creative Center, 2024-2025)

### Best Practice for 5-7 Slide Conversion Carousels

| Slide Position | Treatment |
|---|---|
| All slides (hook through CTA) | Clean, editorial. Same text-on-photo treatment throughout. No logo, no gradient, no watermark. |
| CTA slide — only permitted shift | URL rendered in a brand accent color (e.g., cyan `#00E4DA`) with black outline instead of white. This is subtle enough to read as emphasis, not branding. |

### What NOT to do on the CTA slide

- No logo placement
- No gradient overlays or colored backgrounds
- No brand color blocks
- No suddenly different font or layout
- No elements that don't exist on other TikTok carousels

### What This Replaced

Previously, the procedure called for:
- Nexus logo on the hook slide (Slide 1)
- `nexus-concierge.com` footer watermark on all body slides
- Nexus logo + navy gradient + cyan accents on the CTA slide

This was updated to a fully editorial approach with a single permitted accent (cyan URL) based on the research above.

## Finding: Optimal Hashtag Count for Conversion

### 3-5 Hashtags, Niche-Heavy

- TikTok's Creator Portal recommends 3-5 relevant hashtags.
- 10+ hashtags dilutes algorithmic signal and looks spammy.
- Use a 1:2 or 1:3 ratio of broad to niche tags. Niche tags drive higher-intent traffic.
- Place hashtags at the end of the caption, separated by a line break. Never inline.
- Front-load hook text in the first 80-100 characters (visible preview before "...more").
- TikTok caption limit is 4,000 characters; hashtags count toward it.
- #fyp no longer provides meaningful algorithmic boost.

## Finding: Optimal Slide Count for Conversion

### 5-6 Slides for Link Clicks

- Entertainment/viral carousels perform well at 7-10 slides (dwell time boosts reach).
- Conversion-focused carousels perform better at 4-6 slides (higher completion rate).
- Significant drop-off between slides 5 and 6.
- CTA should land at slide 5-6 to catch users before the steepest engagement cliff.
- Later, HubSpot, and Hootsuite guides cluster around 4-6 slides for action-oriented content.
- Creator consensus: 5 slides for conversion, 7-10 for storytelling/entertainment.
