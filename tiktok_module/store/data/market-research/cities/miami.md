# Miami — Research Bank

Overrated vs. Underrated research for a TikTok carousel. All recommendations sourced from local guides, Reddit threads, and Miami-focused publications (2025-2026).

---

## 1. Beaches

### Overrated
- **South Beach (main strip)** — Overcrowded, selfie-stick chaos, expensive parking, aggressive vendors. Locals almost never go here to actually relax.

### Underrated
- **Crandon Park Beach (Key Biscayne)** — Calm, shallow water, shaded picnic areas, a playground and carousel. This is where Miami families actually spend their weekends. $8 parking, feels like a different world.
- **Virginia Key Beach** — Wilder, more natural feel with dunes and mangroves instead of DJs and umbrellas. Historic site (designated Miami's only beach for Black residents during segregation). Quiet enough to hear cicadas. Great for paddleboarding and kayaking.
- **North Beach (north end of Miami Beach)** — Same gorgeous sand as South Beach, a fraction of the crowds. Locals call it the "quiet side" of the island. Currently being revitalized.
- **South Pointe Park Beach** — The very southern tip of Miami Beach. Watch cruise ships leave the port from here. Local Redditors call it their "happy place" — close to South Beach but a completely different energy.

## 2. Nightlife / Clubs

### Overrated
- **LIV at Fontainebleau** — Local Reddit consensus: "Terrible crowd. Terrible vibe. Rude service. Highly expensive. Not at all worth it." Bottle service-or-bust culture.
- **E11EVEN** — Marketed as a 24-hour ultraclub, but locals warn it's more strip club than dance club. $65+ combined cover for two people, long bouncer lines even with reservations. Polarizing at best.
- **Ocean Drive sidewalk bars** — $22 cocktails, aggressive promoters, "2-for-1" deals that cost $50 after fees. Locals never drink here.

### Underrated
- **Ball & Chain (Little Havana, Calle Ocho)** — Restored 1930s jazz club with free-entry live salsa most nights starting at 8pm. Afro-Cuban, Latin jazz. The most authentically Miami nightlife experience in the city.
- **Dante's HiFi (Wynwood)** — Miami's first vinyl listening bar. Tiny spot on the western fringes of Wynwood with a serious sound system and curated DJ sets. Local DJs and touring selectors. The opposite of bottle-service culture.
- **Brickell bars** — The after-work neighborhood. More low-key than South Beach, more locals per square foot, and drinks that don't require a second mortgage.

## 3. Food / Cuban Food

### Overrated
- **Ocean Drive restaurants (any of them)** — $25 cocktails, automatic 18-20% tips, mediocre food. Locals literally never eat on Ocean Drive.
- **Versailles (as a dining experience)** — Yes, it's iconic and historic (open since 1971). But the food is just okay. Locals say the move is: get a colada at the ventanita (walk-up window), then cross the street to eat at La Carreta, which has a nearly identical menu but better execution.

### Underrated
- **La Carreta (across from Versailles)** — Same style Cuban food, better prepared. Crispy mariquitas, strong mojo. More casual, rural-feeling dining room. This is where locals actually sit down.
- **Islas Canarias** — Family-owned since 1977. Their croquetas are so legendary that other Miami restaurants buy them wholesale. Biting through the crispy shell reveals actual meat and chives, not mystery paste. Also excellent Cuban pastries and entrees.
- **Sanguich** — Possibly the best Cuban sandwich in Miami. Mojo pork, smoked bacon, and city ham all cooked in-house, pressed on Cuban bread from a local bakery. Four locations now, all excellent.
- **Puerto Sagua (South Beach)** — A rare authentic spot actually on the beach. Open since the 1960s, serving late-night Cuban plates to locals and night-shift workers. Kitchen stays open into the early morning hours.
- **La Fresa Francesa (Hialeah)** — Cuban-French fusion worth the drive. Pastelito with foie gras and guayaba, dulce de leche crepes, duck fat gnocchi. A local cult favorite.

## 4. Neighborhoods

### Overrated
- **South Beach / Ocean Drive** — Great for one photo walk. Terrible for spending actual time and money. Overpriced everything, aggressive vendors, tourist-density that makes it hard to enjoy.
- **Bayside Marketplace** — Feels like a mall with a waterfront view. Mostly chain stores and overpriced tourist fare.

### Underrated
- **Little Havana** — Skip the guided tours. Walk it yourself: cafecito at a ventanita, watch dominos at Maximo Gomez Park (Domino Park), peek into cigar shops, stay for live music at night.
- **Coconut Grove** — Miami's oldest neighborhood. Bohemian, walkable, with a farmer's market, Michelin-starred restaurants, dive bars, and waterfront strolls near CocoWalk. Currently in a renaissance.
- **Wynwood** — Beyond the Instagram walls: a dense concentration of bars, galleries, and food spots. The most walkable nightlife neighborhood in Miami.
- **Coral Gables** — "The City Beautiful." Mediterranean architecture, tree-lined boulevards, Miracle Mile shopping. Feels like a different city entirely.
- **Key Biscayne** — Quiet island minutes from downtown via the Rickenbacker Causeway. Two parks, water on all sides. Where locals go to decompress.

## 5. Coffee / Brunch

### Overrated
- **GreenStreet Cafe (Coconut Grove)** — The quintessential "see and be seen" spot with long weekend waits. Large portions but middling quality. Gayot rated it 11/20. More scene than substance.
- **Any brunch on Ocean Drive** — Same story as dinner: overpriced, underwhelming, tourist-focused.

### Underrated
- **Panther Coffee (multiple locations, originated in Wynwood)** — Small-batch roasting, rich espresso. The local coffee standard. Multiple locations but still feels independent.
- **Cafe Demetrio (Coral Gables)** — Miami's oldest coffeehouse, open since 1997. Extensive coffee menu plus Belgian Monte Cristo sandwiches and full breakfast. Old-school local energy.
- **Cafe Bastille (Miami Beach)** — Walk-in only, true local vibe steps from the beach. During peak hours you'll spot neighbors and local baristas — that's how you know it's legit.
- **La Fresa Francesa (Hialeah)** — Does double duty as brunch: Cuban-French pastries and creative plates. Worth the 20-minute drive from the beach.
- **B Bistro + Bakery (Brickell)** — From pastry legend Antonio Bachour. Pastelito Pancakes, Croissant French Toast, Mexican Avocado Toast. Polished but not pretentious.

## 6. Views / Sunset Spots

### Overrated
- **South Beach sunset from Ocean Drive** — You're facing east. The sunset is behind you. Tourists sit here waiting for a sunset they'll never see from this angle. (The sunrise is great here, but nobody's awake for it.)

### Underrated
- **Venetian Causeway** — One of the most underrated sunset spots in Miami. Bridge architecture + skyline + water. Walk or bike it.
- **South Pointe Park (at sunset)** — Same views as South Beach without the crowds. Watch the sky change over Government Cut as cruise ships pass.
- **The Rusty Pelican (Virginia Key)** — Restaurant with massive windows facing downtown Miami. The sunset paints the entire skyline gold. Worth going just for a drink at golden hour.
- **Maurice Gibb Memorial Park (Sunset Harbour)** — Tiny park with unobstructed bay views. Perfect for photography as city lights begin to twinkle.
- **Hobie Island Beach Park** — Known for windsurfing, but locals come here at dusk for wide-open bayside sunset views most tourists never discover.

## 7. Activities / Day Plans

### Overrated
- **Bayside Marketplace boat tours** — Overpriced narrated cruises past celebrity homes. You'll see gates and hedges, not celebrities.
- **Guided Little Havana walking tours** — $40-60 to be herded through Calle Ocho. You can do the same walk for free and stop wherever you want.
- **Renting a Lamborghini on Ocean Drive** — Peak tourist behavior. Locals cringe. You'll spend most of the time in traffic or looking for parking.

### Underrated
- **Self-guided Little Havana evening** — Cafecito at a ventanita, dominos at Maximo Gomez Park, cigar shop browsing, dinner, then live salsa at Ball & Chain. Free to walk, and you set your own pace.
- **Kayak or paddleboard at Virginia Key** — Calm bay water, mangrove trails, and a fraction of the cost of organized tours. Rentals available on-site.
- **Bike the Venetian Causeway at sunset** — Flat, scenic, and you get the best skyline-over-water views in the city. Rentals from Citi Bike stations.
- **Wynwood gallery + bar crawl on foot** — Skip the Wynwood Walls tour bus. Walk the side streets where the real murals and galleries are, then bar-hop. Dante's HiFi for music, then wherever the night takes you.
- **Spend a full day on Key Biscayne** — Morning at Crandon Park Beach, lunch at a local spot, afternoon at Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park (with the historic lighthouse). Most tourists never cross the causeway.

## Notes

- **Miami faces east.** This is the single biggest thing tourists get wrong about sunsets. The beach faces the Atlantic — sunrises are spectacular, sunsets require going to the bay side or a causeway.
- **Little Havana is best at night.** Daytime is fine for the walk-through, but the live music and energy come alive after dark, especially at Ball & Chain.
- **Hialeah is slept on.** It's 80%+ Cuban-American, deeply authentic, and has some of the best food in greater Miami. La Fresa Francesa is the tip of the iceberg.
- **Versailles ventanita hack** — The walk-up window is the real Versailles experience. Order a colada (tiny cups of strong, sweet Cuban coffee to share) for under $2. Skip the sit-down dining room.
- **Key Biscayne requires a car (or a long Uber).** The Rickenbacker Causeway toll is $2.25. Worth it, but factor in the logistics.
