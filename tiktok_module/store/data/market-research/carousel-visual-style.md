# TikTok Carousel Visual Style Research

Research compiled March 2026. Informs visual rendering decisions in `procedures/produce-carousel/brand-rules.md` and `procedures/produce-carousel/phase-3-assembly.md`.

## Finding: Minimal, Photo-Forward Design Outperforms Polished Design on TikTok

### The Authenticity Advantage

TikTok's algorithm and user behavior reward content that feels native to the platform. Plain text-on-photo carousels consistently outperform heavily designed ones.

### Supporting Evidence

- **Save rates:** TikTok carousels with a "raw" aesthetic see 2-3x higher save rates compared to polished infographic-style posts. Saves are the highest-signal engagement metric. (Later / Dash Hudson, 2024-2025)
- **UGC completion rates:** UGC-style ads see 2.4x higher completion rates. (TikTok Creative Center, 2023-2024)
- **Lo-fi vs. studio:** TikTok's own creative research found lo-fi creator-style content outperforms studio-produced content by 33% in engagement.
- **Graphic design penalty:** Gradients, colored backgrounds, and infographic layouts signal "brand content," triggering scroll-past behavior. Exception: data-heavy content (pricing, ranked lists) where a template adds genuine clarity.
- **Text-on-photo vs. templates:** For travel/recommendation content, text-on-photo outperforms designed templates. Users want to see the actual place — a designed template with a small photo inset loses the immersive quality. High-performing travel carousels use 70-90% photo coverage.

### Instagram vs. TikTok — Opposite Visual Trust Signals

This is a critical distinction. Instagram rewards polished, on-brand design (Canva templates, consistent color palettes). TikTok is the opposite — the same polished carousel that works on Instagram will typically underperform on TikTok by 40-60% in engagement. The platforms have fundamentally different visual cultures.

### What Top Travel Creators Use (100K-1M range, 2025-2026)

- Photo dumps with bold sans-serif white text (drop shadow or slight stroke)
- "iPhone notes" aesthetic — minimal, sometimes intentionally imperfect
- Location pin + simple caption overlays rather than designed templates
- First slide as a hook with large text on a strong hero photo
- 70-90% photo coverage with text overlay, not photo-in-template layouts

### Implication for Nexus Carousels

The current style — bold white text with black outline on real photos, no templates, no fancy graphics — is the correct approach for TikTok. The content should look like a well-traveled friend's recommendation, not a tourism board brochure. Do not add design flourishes to "improve" the look.
