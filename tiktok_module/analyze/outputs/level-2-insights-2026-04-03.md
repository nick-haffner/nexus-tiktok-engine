## Phase 1: Dimension Review

### Content Strategy Assessment

The clearest signal is **content type**: carousels average 10.9K views versus 327 for videos — a 33:1 ratio at high confidence (n=27 vs n=18). Per carousel-visual-style.md, TikTok's algorithm rewards native-feeling content; carousels with text-on-photo match this pattern while videos in the dataset are early-era, pre-strategy content. Videos show higher engagement rates (5.45% vs 3.94%) but this is a small-audience resonance effect, not a quality signal.

### Framework Signal

Local vs Tourist leads at 53.7K mean views (n=4), Worth It at 10.7K (n=2), The 24-Hour Test at 799 (n=1). The direction is suggestive — Local vs Tourist contains 3 of the top 5 all-time posts — but with only 7 classified posts, confidence is low. A backfill of the 38 unclassified posts would substantially sharpen this comparison.

### City Patterns

Dallas shows a **declining trajectory** across 6 posts — early posts performed well (54K, 2.8K) but the most recent (687, 183) are well below median. Los Angeles underperforms: 4 posts averaging 774 views with the lowest engagement of any multi-post city (2.13%). Seattle (4 posts, 24K average, improving trajectory) and Phoenix (2 posts, 34.6K average) are bright spots. The recent content mix is 30% Dallas, 30% LA — the two weakest multi-post cities are getting the most attention.

### Slide Count

Longer carousels outperform on views: 9+ slides average 26.5K (n=6), 6-8 average 11.3K (n=11), 1-5 average 648 (n=28). The 1-5 bucket is inflated by videos. Among carousels, 9+ has a declining trajectory while 6-8 is stable.

### What Can't Be Assessed

hook_style, content_topics, and sound_type have 0% coverage. These are the highest-leverage creative variables that remain unmeasurable. Until tagged during production, "which hooks work?" and "which topics drive saves?" are unanswerable.

## Phase 2: Outlier Investigation

**Seattle (91K views):** copy.md reveals a straightforward Local vs Tourist hook — "Local vs Tourist spots SEATTLE." The copy uses specific recommendations (Ballard & Fremont coffee shops vs. Original Starbucks) with short, punchy contrast lines. Each slide is one category with a clear tourist trap and local alternative. The structure is clean and each slide earns its place.

**Phoenix (63K views):** Similar structure — "Phoenix: Local Spots vs Tourist Traps." Copy uses hyper-specific local knowledge (Sonoran hot dog stands, Roosevelt Row First Friday art walk). Slide 5 appears to be image-only (no text overlay), which breaks the pattern — worth investigating whether this hurt or helped.

**Dallas-01-21 (54K views):** "Local vs Tourist in Dallas" with an emoji hook. Copy includes a cross-city recommendation (Fort Worth Stockyards) that expands the carousel's scope beyond the city name. This "adjacent destination" pattern is unique to this post and may contribute to its performance.

**Bottom performers** (dallas-2025-09-19, rome-2025-09-17, west-coast-2026-02-03-4175) lack content artifacts. No creative investigation possible — noted as addressable gap.

**Pattern across top 3:** All use Local vs Tourist. All use specific, named recommendations rather than generic categories. All have short, decisive copy — 1-2 lines per point. Per carousel-branding-and-conversion.md, this aligns with the "earn then ask" principle — editorial content builds trust before the CTA.
