## Audience Health

The engagement quality grid splits almost evenly: 12 Star, 12 Niche Hit, 11 Viral Shallow, 10 Underperformer. The "Star" skew label is technically correct but misleading — the account doesn't have a dominant content personality yet. It produces star-quality content (Seattle 91K at 7.88% engagement) and viral-shallow content (Dallas-01-21 54K at 2.99%) in roughly equal measure. Per carousel-branding-and-conversion.md, native-feeling content outperforms branded content 2-3x — the Star posts likely feel more native than the Viral Shallow ones.

## Save Rate as Growth Proxy

Save rate correlates **positively** with follower conversion (though driven almost entirely by Austin). The 13 high-save posts (>0.8% save rate) cluster in carousels (11 of 13) and in Local vs Tourist (4 of 5 classified high-save posts). Dallas and Seattle each contribute 2 high-save posts. This aligns with Level 2's finding that Local vs Tourist leads on views — it also leads on saves.

## Return Audience Trend

FYP% has **increased** from ~45-55% for early posts to 90-100% recently. This is the opposite of healthy growth. Early posts had substantial non-FYP traffic (profile, search), but recent posts are almost entirely algorithmically distributed. Caveat: the early low-FYP readings may be a small-denominator artifact (100-300 views makes a few profile visits register as high percentage). Recent high-FYP readings at thousands of views are more reliable.
