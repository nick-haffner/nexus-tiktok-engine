# Phase 4: Title, Caption, Hashtags & Posting

You are Claude, preparing the final posting assets. The goal is to generate the ChatGPT prompt for the title, caption, and hashtags, then guide the manager through posting.

## Phase 4 Acceptance Criteria

Before posting, the manager should verify:

- [ ] Is there a short, engaging title for the TikTok post?
- [ ] Is there a caption of at least 2-3 sentences with relevant emojis?
- [ ] Are there hashtags mixing broad reach and city-specific tags?
- [ ] Has the sound been reviewed and swapped for a trending sound if needed?

---

## Step 4.1 — Generate the ChatGPT Caption Prompt

Claude generates a ready-to-paste prompt for the manager to use in ChatGPT. The prompt should include:

- The full approved slide text from `copy.md`.
- The city and framework context.
- Any meta-tips or advice that didn't fit on the slides (e.g., traffic timing, pro tips) — these are ideal caption content.
- Instructions for ChatGPT to produce:
  - **Title:** Short, attention-grabbing. This appears in the TikTok post listing — it is separate from the hook slide text.
  - **Caption:** At least 2-3 sentences. Longer captions help with the TikTok algorithm. Must include emojis (sparkles, stars, or context-appropriate like ☕ for coffee, 🌴 for a beach city). Should sell the carousel, encourage saves/shares, and include any bonus tips that didn't make the slides.
  - **Hashtags:** A mix of broad-reach tags (#travel, #localtips, #traveltiktok) and city-specific tags (#losangeles, #lathingstodo). Aim for 5-10 hashtags.
- Instruct ChatGPT to generate a single best recommendation with all three sections (title, caption, hashtags) — not multiple options.

Tell the manager: *"Here is the prompt to paste into ChatGPT to generate your title, caption, and hashtags. Review the output, request revisions in ChatGPT if needed, and save the final version to `caption.md` in the carousel directory. Then proceed to posting."*

> **MANAGER ACTION:** Copy the ChatGPT prompt. Open ChatGPT, paste it, review the output, revise if needed. Save the final title/caption/hashtags to `caption.md` in the carousel directory.

## Step 4.2 — Sound Selection

This is a manual step in TikTok. Claude cannot assist directly.

> **MANAGER ACTION (in TikTok):**
>
> 1. When uploading the carousel images, TikTok will suggest a sound based on the content.
> 2. Review the suggested sound — if it doesn't suit the carousel, change it.
> 3. Check the "Popular" section in the sound menu for trending sounds that could boost reach.
> 4. Adding a sound always helps with the algorithm — don't post without one.

## Step 4.3 — Post

> **MANAGER ACTION:**
>
> 1. Upload the final images from `final-slides/` to TikTok as a carousel.
> 2. Add the selected title, caption, and hashtags.
> 3. Apply the selected sound.
> 4. Post.
> 5. Update the carousel's `README.md` with the post date. Check back later to add view counts and performance notes.
